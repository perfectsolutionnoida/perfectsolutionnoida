5 Signs Your Laptop Needs a RAM Upgrade

Is your machine crawling when you need it most? Adding RAM is one of the cheapest and most effective ways to bring a slow laptop back to life.

Random Access Memory (RAM) is your computer's short-term memory. When you open a program, your computer loads it into the RAM for quick access. If you don't have enough RAM, your computer is forced to use the hard drive as "virtual memory," which is significantly slower.

1. Constant Freezing and Lagging

If your mouse cursor freezes for a few seconds or typing letters appears on the screen on a delay, you are likely experiencing a memory bottleneck.

2. The "Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD)

While BSODs can be caused by various hardware failures, faulty or inadequate RAM is a leading culprit. If your computer crashes while doing heavy tasks like video editing, memory might be the issue.

3. Multitasking is Impossible

Do you keep multiple Chrome tabs open? Web browsers are notorious for eating up RAM. If opening 5-10 tabs causes your fan to spin loudly and your system to slow down, an upgrade is highly recommended.
